Prep: 10 mins | Cook: 15 mins | Servings: 5

Difficulty: Moderate

Cuisine: Chinese

Easy mapo tofu recipe that creates the authentic taste of China that features soft tofu cooked in a rich, spicy, and savory sauce that is full of aroma. Serve it over steamed

Credit: REDhouse spice

Ingredients:

10 oz (120 g) ground pork
5 teaspoons Shaoxing wine
2.5 teaspoon light soy sauce
1.25 teaspoon minced ginger
2.5 teaspoon cornstarch
5 teaspoons Sichuan
2.5 tablespoon vegetable oil
7.5 tablespoons Doubanjiang
5 green onion , chopped
2.5 block firm or medium firm tofu
2.5 cup chicken stock (or water)
5 teaspoons homemade chili oil
0.63 teaspoon five-spice powder
2.5 teaspoon sugar

Method:

Step 1

Combine ground meat, cooking wine, soy sauce, and ginger in a small bowl. Mix well.

Step 2

Combine cornstarch with 1 tablespoon of water in a small bowl. Mix well and set aside.

Step 3

Heat the oil and Sichuan peppercorns in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. When the Sichuan peppercorns turn dark brown and crispy, scoop them out with a spatula and transfer into a bowl layered with paper towels to soak extra oil. Save to use for garnishing the dish (Optional).

Step 4

Add the ground meat and Doubanjiang. Cook over medium heat and chop the ground meat into small bits with a spatula, until pork is evenly coated with Doubanjiang and fully cooked through. Add green onion and stir fry for another minute.

Step 5

Spread tofu evenly on top of ground pork . Add chili oil, five-spice powder, and sugar. Pour in the broth and cook until brought to a simmer. Simmer, covered, over medium-low heat for 8 to 10 minutes, or until the sauce has reduced to half the original amount. Taste the tofu with some broth (be careful, it will be very hot!). Adjust seasoning by adding salt if needed. If the dish is too spicy, add another teaspoon of sugar to balance it out. Gently mix well with spatula.

Step 6

(Optional) Meanwhile, grind the fried Sichuan peppercorns (you used when heating up the oil) in a coffee grinder or using mortar and pestle.

Step 7

Mix cornstarch water again until fully dissolved and swirl it into the skillet. Gently stir a few times with a spatula, until sauce thickens. Turn off heat and transfer everything to a bowl.

Step 8

Garnish with extra green onion and a small pinch of the ground Sichuan peppercorns

Step 9

Serve hot and enjoy!
