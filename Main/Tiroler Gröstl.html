Prep: 30 mins | Cook: 30 mins | Servings: 3- 4

Difficulty: Easy

Cuisine: Austrian

The Tiroler Gröstl is comfort food at its best! Among the most common traditional Austrian food in Vienna, it is more common in the Austrian Alps. The rich dish is also simple and is a pan-fried dish of potato, chopped beef or pork with lots of butter and onion. Often topped with a fried egg

Credit: Austria

Ingredients:

11 ⁄ 2 tablespoon corn, sunflower or vegetable oil
400 g / 14 oz smoked bacon lardons (or cooked ham)
1 onion, cut into chunks
500 g / 1lb cooked potatoes, cold, cut into small chunks
1 tablespoon caraway seeds
1 1 ⁄ 2 tablespoon hot sweet paprika
Small handful of parsley, roughly chopped

Method:

Step 1

Heat the oil in a large frying pan, then fry the bacon and onion together for 10 minutes until the bacon is golden.

Step 2

Lift it out of the pan and set aside. Now add the potatoes to the pan and let them fry for about 10 minutes until golden. Sprinkle the caraway and paprika, season well, then fry for another minute, stirring to release their fragrance.

Step 3

Lift it out of the pan and set aside. Now add the potatoes to the pan and let them fry for about 10 minutes until golden. Sprinkle the caraway and paprika, season well, then fry for another minute, stirring to release their fragrance.

Step 4

Serve hot and enjoy!
