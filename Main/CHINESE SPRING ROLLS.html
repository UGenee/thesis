Prep: 30 mins | Cook: 15 mins | Servings: 15

Difficulty: Easy

Cuisine: Chinese

A traditional food popular in many regions of China, spring rolls (Chun Juan, 春卷), aka egg rolls in American English, are one of the most sought after dishes in overseas Chinese restaurants.

Credit: REDhouse spice

Ingredients:

15 large flour-based spring roll wrappers
7oz minced pork (or chicken/beef)
4 oz shrimp, shelled and cubed
2 cups carrot, grated
2 cups bean sprouts
2 cups Chinese chives, cut into sections
6 shiitake mushrooms - thinly sliced
2 stalks scallions, finely chopped
100 g dried mung bean vermicelli, rehydrated and cut into sections
2 eggs
2 tablespoon oyster sauce
1 teaspoon sesame oil
½ teaspoon salt
¼ teaspoon ground white or black pepper
Cooking oil

Method:

Step 1

Mix the filling. Put all the ingredients for the filling into a large mixing bowl (NB: leave out one egg yolk for brushing the wrappers).

Step 2

Add all the seasonings. Mix until well combined (It’s best to do so with your hands. Wear kitchen gloves if available).

Step 3

Assemble the rolls. Gently separate the spring roll wrappers one by one. Remember to always cover them with a clean damp cloth as they dry out easily.

Step 4

Place one wrapper on a flat surface with one of the four corners facing towards you. Put about 3 tablespoon of the filling above the closest corner (Use a spoon to help shape it into a rectangle).

Step 5

Lift the closest corner to wrap the filling then roll it to the half-line of the wrapper. Try to wrap it as tight as possible without leaving any air pockets inside. But be gentle during this process to avoid tearing.

Step 6

Lightly press two ends of the filling then fold the left and right corners of the wrapper towards the middle.

Step 7

Roll the cylinder until only a triangle is left at the top. Brush a layer of egg yolk over the triangle. Roll again to seal completely. Repeat to assemble all the rolls.

Step 8

Deep fry the rolls. Heat the oil for deep frying until it reaches 180°C/350°F. Gently slide in the rolls one by one (Do not overcrowd. Fry them in batches if necessary).

Step 9

Fry over medium heat. Flip over a few times to ensure even browning. When they become golden, transfer out to a tray lined with kitchen paper to absorb excess oil.

Step 10

Serve hot and enjoy!
